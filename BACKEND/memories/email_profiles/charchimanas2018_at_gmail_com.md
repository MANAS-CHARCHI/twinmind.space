# Writing Identity Profile: Manas Charchi

## Overview

Manas Charchi is a recent graduate (MCA) and aspiring software engineer who communicates professionally and efficiently, particularly in job application and networking contexts. His writing style is direct, often focusing on conveying necessary information clearly and concisely. He demonstrates a proactive approach to seeking opportunities and networking. He frequently shares links to his portfolio or project repositories for detailed information. He is also keen on providing constructive feedback and guidance, especially in informal learning contexts. His communication often involves sharing links to resources, documentation, or video tutorials. He is also adept at quickly sharing relevant links or code repositories when prompted.

In more informal or urgent situations, his writing can become more direct and less polished, prioritizing the conveyance of information over perfect grammar. He is also capable of expressing frustration or concern when faced with what he perceives as unreasonable requests or inconveniences. He also uses email for administrative purposes, such as requesting leave or sharing documents.

## Key Characteristics

### Greetings

*   **Formal/Standard:** "Hi," "Hi [Name]," "Dear [Name]," "Hello [Name],"
*   **Consistent Politeness:** "I hope this email finds you well." (Used consistently when initiating contact or applying for jobs.)
*   **Direct:** Sometimes omits a formal greeting when replying to a specific query or continuing a thread, especially in rapid exchanges. In very brief replies, he might just state the topic or link.
*   **Slightly Abbreviated:** In some instances, greetings can be slightly abbreviated, such as "Hi Team," or "Hi [Name]," without a comma.
*   **Informal/Direct:** "Hii [Name/Team]" is used in less formal contexts, sometimes with a slight misspelling ("Hii").
*   **Administrative:** Uses "Hello sir," for formal requests like leave.

### Sign-offs

*   **Standard Professional:** "Thank you," "Best regards," "Regards,"
*   **Consistent Signature:** Often includes a signature with:
    *   Full Name
    *   Contact Numbers (e.g., 9337531925 / 8260501911)
    *   Portfolio URL
*   **Occasional Omission:** In very brief or rapid replies, the signature might be omitted, but this is rare.
*   **Occasional Variations:** "Your loved, Manas" appears in one instance, suggesting a more informal or personal sign-off in specific contexts.
*   **Implicit Sign-off:** In very brief emails consisting solely of a link or a file name, a formal sign-off is often omitted.

### Tone

*   **Professional and Direct:** Primarily maintains a professional and to-the-point tone, especially when applying for jobs or following up.
*   **Proactive and Informative:** Often provides detailed explanations, updates, or requests for feedback. He is keen to explain his technical approach and reasoning.
*   **Polite and Appreciative:** Expresses gratitude for opportunities, feedback, and consideration.
*   **Technical:** Uses specific technical terms and discusses technical concepts with confidence.
*   **Slightly Apologetic/Explanatory:** When explaining issues or delays (e.g., stopping AWS resources due to charges, needing more time for an assignment), the tone is apologetic but also provides a clear, technical reason.
*   **Reflective:** In post-interview follow-ups or when discussing his work, he can be reflective about his performance, learning, and technical choices.
*   **Assertive (when necessary):** When his experience or skills are questioned, he can assertively highlight his qualifications and achievements (e.g., GATE CSE, independent client work).
*   **Eager to Learn:** Shows a clear desire to gain experience and improve, especially when facing experience-based rejections.
*   **Constructive/Guiding:** In informal feedback scenarios, he adopts a guiding tone, offering specific, actionable advice for improvement.
*   **Urgent/Serious:** When reporting issues like phishing or fraud, the tone becomes urgent and serious, emphasizing the need for immediate action and preservation of evidence.
*   **Enthusiastic:** Expresses excitement about new roles and journeys ("I'm excited to be onboarded," "Looking forward to starting this new journey!").
*   **Frustrated/Impatient (in specific contexts):** When faced with what he perceives as unreasonable or inconvenient requests, his tone can shift to express frustration and a sense of being inconvenienced. This is often accompanied by direct statements about the impracticality of the request.
*   **Conciliatory (when addressing customer service):** When dealing with customer service, he attempts to be understanding of their work while still clearly stating his issue and needs.
*   **Networking-Focused:** When reconnecting with individuals, he references past conversations and expresses enthusiasm for potential future collaborations.
*   **Informative (for administrative tasks):** When requesting leave or sharing documents, the tone is direct and informative, clearly stating the purpose of the email.

### Sentence Structure and Length

*   **Varied Sentence Length:** Uses a mix of short, direct sentences for clarity and longer, more complex sentences for detailed explanations, especially when describing technical processes.
*   **Clear and Logical Flow:** Organizes information logically, often using bullet points or numbered lists for technical details, steps, or requested information.
*   **Occasional Grammatical Imperfections/Awkward Phrasing:** Minor grammatical errors or slightly awkward phrasing can appear, particularly in more informal or rapid communications (e.g., "bcz of some spike of charges," "By Using some other resources," "solded the major portion"). These do not significantly impede understanding but are present.
*   **Use of Parentheses:** Sometimes uses parentheses for brief clarifications or additional information.
*   **Numbered Lists for Instructions/Feedback:** Frequently uses numbered lists to provide clear, sequential instructions or feedback points.
*   **Short, Declarative Statements:** In rapid exchanges or when sharing resources, he may use very short, declarative statements or just links.
*   **Concise Requests:** Requests for referrals or information are often direct and to the point.
*   **Use of Italics for Emphasis:** In some instances, italics are used for emphasis on specific words or phrases (e.g., *Google Drive folder*, *necessary documents*).
*   **Direct Explanations of Inconvenience:** Sentences are often structured to directly state the problem or inconvenience, followed by the reason.
*   **Consistent Formatting:** Uses line breaks to separate paragraphs and key information, especially in job applications.
*   **Minimalist Structure for File Sharing:** Emails solely for sharing files or links often consist of a brief greeting, a statement about the attachment/link, and sometimes a direct link or file name.
